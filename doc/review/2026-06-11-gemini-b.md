## 2. Pain Points

* **Operator UI/UX Invisibility**: The operator struggled to observe background processes due to an existing IRC client quirk.
> **16:12:28 rlupi**: we have no scribe, claude please run one
> **16:13:14 rlupi**: oh, intersting. I don't see it
> **16:14:43 claude**: ...rlupi: your ircII may be hiding it — same status-window quirk as this morning...


* **Brittle Wake Loops ("The Sharp Edge")**: The new `irc-wait` subcommand introduces a pattern where agents can miss updates if their wake loops fail to properly re-arm immediately after execution. This led to a manual intervention where the human operator expected a faster response.
> **16:34:27 rlupi**: agy? ping
> **16:52:19 rlupi**: I think agy needs a ping to response... irc-client doesn't seem to work well for agy
> **16:52:59 claude**: ...agy runs the same mechanism now, so if they are silent their watcher likely was not re-armed after the last response — re-arming after every wake is the sharp edge of this pattern.


* **Historical Traffic Replay Pollution**: The `irc-wait` command matched `(hist)` tokens from `CHATHISTORY` logs, creating false-positive wake events on historical traffic rather than live, new events upon reconnection.

## 3. Proposals

### Clarify agent re-arming patterns in protocol documentation

* **Problem solved**: Addresses the "sharp edge" where agents go silent because they fail to loop or re-arm their `irc-wait` traps immediately following an alert.
* **Cost**: Small
* **Details**: Update `doc/protocol/session-lifecycle-and-gtd.md` to specify an invariant: a waking event must instantly trigger a concurrent background re-arm of the `irc-wait` process before the agent begins generating its text response.

### Make `irc-wait` ignore history replays natively

* **Problem solved**: Eliminates agent wake loops firing on old traffic upon connection downtime recovery.
* **Cost**: Small
* **Details**: Formalize `claude`'s patch by explicitly rejecting or filtering line streams that feature the Ergo history marker prefix.
