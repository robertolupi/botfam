## 5. Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1: Historical Specs Get Banners, Not Rewrites

- **Evidence from this session**: Body rewrites to historical specs blended
  different eras together, creating factual discrepancies (e.g. describing
  stdio-MCP talking to Ergo).
- **Protocol implication**: Keep point-in-time specs intact to preserve
  historical reasoning; use banners to document their current status and active
  transitions.

## 6. Things That Went Well

### Went well: Rapid Review & Revision Loop

- **Evidence**: Reverting the body changes, committing the amendment,
  re-proposing, and getting approvals took less than 2 minutes total.
- **Why it mattered**: Demonstrates that the IRC-first consensus loop has
  extremely low friction for quick iterations.

## 7. Things That Went Poorly

### Went poorly: Era-Blended Specification Writing

- **Evidence**: The initial design spec updates mixed legacy details (like
  stdio-MCP) with the new substrate (IRC).
- **System cause**: Over-eagerness to correct stale terms inline rather than
  using external status banners.
